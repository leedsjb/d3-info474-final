Air Traffic in the National Airspace System

Every year, the United States National Airspace System makes it possible for just under one billion passengers to reach their destinations. Although the majority of those passengers arrive on time, frustrating delays still happen. In fiscal year 2016, 211,966 flights were delayed costing an estimated 22.5 billion dollars in lost revenues and increased expenses.
